EVA Setup Guide 




To set up a job in EVA, you will need: 

• An offline copy of the email with the job request 
• The Instruction (usually attached in the email as a .pdf or .docx) 
• Images of the damaged vehicle 

Make sure you have downloaded all three of the above. Once you have these, you can 
begin to setup the job in EVA for the engineers to complete. 

If any of these are missing, immediately pause and locate them. If you have difficulty locating 
anything, just ask for help. 



1) Begin Setup 

Open The Instruction so you can reference it – 
it contains key details that you will copy. 

In EVA, click "Create New Inspection" to open 
the Inspection Setup T ool. The Inspection 
Setup T ool will go page-by-page and prompt 
you to fill in fields to create the database entry. 
We only use a small number of the available 
fields while leaving many of them empty, so it's 
important to pay attention to which fields need 
filling in. 







2) Go Through Setup 



Page 1) 

• "Reg No" Input the vehicle registration 
here. 

• Afterwards, click the Experian logo to 
load information about the vehicle.

possible then the image should be discarded. If all the images are full of reflections and can’t 
be cropped, then just choose “Image-based Assessment”

## Page 4

4) Complete Remaining Fields 

Now the initial setup is complete, but you still need to make some changes to the database 
entry. 




Overview Tab: 

• “Request” Change this to the date we received the email in our inbox for the job.

## Page 5

Vehicle Tab: 

• “Speedo” Enter the mileage of the vehicle. If this is present in one of the images of the 
vehicle, then copy what the dashboards says in the image. If this is not present in any of the 
images, you can get an estimate of the mileage on Percayso by entering the VRM and 
accident date. 




• “Eng Notes on Vehicle” You can press F9 in this field to access a list of presets. You only 
need to enter info in this field if: 

• The vehicle is a previous total loss. To verify this, you need to read the “VRM Notes”. 
If the vehicle is a previous total loss, you need to verify that the date of the total loss 
is not the same (or very similar to) the date of the accident, as this would indicate that 
the VRM Notes are for the current case, not a previous case. 

• The mileage (in the “Speedo” field) has been estimated based on MOT data, rather 
than being verified by reading it from an image of the dashboard.

## Page 6

Valuation Tab: 

• Glass’s “Retail” and “Trade” Enter an estimate of the vehicle’s retail and trade value in 
these two fields in the top half of the page. To get these estimates, enter the VRM, mileage 
and incident date on Glasses. 

• Percayso “Retail” and “Trade” Enter an estimate of the vehicle’s retail and trade value in 
these two fields in the top half of the page. To get these estimates, enter the VRM, mileage 
and incident date on Percayso. 

• “Retail”, “Trade” and “Engineer Value” Look which estimate method provided the 
highest single value and then carry down that pair of values into the below fields. You then 
also need to input the highest single value into the “Engineer Value” field.

## Page 7

Photos Tab: 

Finally, you need to upload the vehicle damage images into the photos tab. There is an 
important order in which the images need to be uploaded: 
• The first two images you upload are handled separately by EVA. The first two images 
should be one image of the overall vehicle showing the reg, and a closeup image of 
the damaged area. 
• After you have uploaded the first two images, you then need to drag in ALL of the 
images (including the two you just uploaded). This may seem counterintuitive, but it’s 
important that the first two images are duplicated. 

5) Save and Complete Box upload 

You have now completed the setup process in EVA! To save your changes, you need to click 
the traffic light symbol in the top right corner. After moving on, make sure you have 
everything uploaded to the Box folder for the engineer to access. Remember, you need:  

• An offline copy of the email with the job request 
• The Instruction (usually attached in the email as a .pdf or .docx) 
• Images of the damaged vehicle 

You can also carry forward any notes for the engineer needed to help them complete the job 
in the form of boxnotes.

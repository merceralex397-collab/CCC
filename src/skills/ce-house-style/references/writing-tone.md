# CE Writing Tone

Use this reference when drafting or revising Collision Engineers written output.

## Register

- Write as a practical engineering business: direct, neutral, factual, and source-led.
- Prefer short sentences and concrete findings.
- Use professional confidence where the evidence supports it; avoid hedging by default.
- Keep conclusions tied to the evidence reviewed.
- Avoid inflated legalese unless the document type requires formal CPR-style wording.

## Do

- State what was reviewed.
- State what was found.
- Explain the engineering or valuation reason.
- Separate evidence, judgement, and limitations.
- Use "we consider", "the evidence indicates", and "on the information provided" where a judgement needs a boundary.

## Avoid

- AI tell-tales such as "it is important to note", "delve", "comprehensive", or generic caveat stacking.
- Internal workflow terms in external reports, including "EVA", "guide uplift", "prompt", "mode", "AI", "tool output", and "draft strategy".
- Criticising the instructed party or opposing expert personally.
- Unsupported absolutes where evidence is incomplete.

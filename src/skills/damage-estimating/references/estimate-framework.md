# CE Estimate Framework

Compact estimating doctrine for `damage-estimating`. Use `estimate-framework-full.md` only when a case needs the detailed doctrine or provenance trace.

## Failure Modes To Guard Against

- Thin output: visible damage is listed without inferred structural, trim, sensor, or adjacent-panel implications.
- Zero-parts estimate: repair labour appears but no clips, brackets, mouldings, lamps, sensors, or consumables are considered.
- Under-replacement: distorted alloy, split bumper, cracked lamp, deployed restraint, or safety-critical item is repaired without support.
- Wrong-side error: nearside/offside or front/rear copied incorrectly from source material.
- Missed propagation: impact-path damage does not follow into co-located fragile items.

## Completeness Walkthrough

1. Confirm side and point of impact.
2. Identify outer panels.
3. Identify lamps, glass, mouldings, trims, sensors, cameras, grilles, badges, and clips.
4. Check wheel, tyre, suspension, steering, geometry, and alignment.
5. Check airbag, SRS, seatbelt, and pretensioner indicators.
6. Check ADAS calibration need.
7. Check air-conditioning, cooling pack, radiator, condenser, intercooler, and fans.
8. Check underbody, sill, floor, crossmember, crash bar, and reinforcement.
9. Decide repair, replace, blend, or inspect.
10. Add labour, paint, parts, extras, and VAT treatment.
11. Record assumptions and evidence gaps.

Use the full framework only when a case needs detailed doctrine or an evaluation trace.
